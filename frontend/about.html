Reimagining the College Search

Prospective college students often base their college search off of the reputation of the school, desired programs, career outcomes, etc. Students often don't consider the mental health resources offered on campus when applying to college. Mental health affects 73% of students during their time in college, leading to lower GPAs, higher drop out rates, and feelings of hopelessness. The Mental Health Initiative is determined to change the way students pursue their college search, making it easy for one to find data on their school's resources and student wellness in seconds.

State of Mental Illness on Campus

Colleges are struggling to meet the demand for mental health services on campus. Nationally, 40% of students experience depression, while one in three students reported to have anxiety, and one in seven said they have thought of suicide in the past year.

Mental Health Issues Affecting College Students

Depression, Anxiety, Eating Disorders, Addiction, etc. are affecting students across the world. These mental health issues put a toll in student's energy levels, concentration, mental ability and hinder performance.

Mental Health Resources on Campus Matters

Adding more mental health services has been shown to improve student academic performance, improve student mental wellbeing, prevent suicides, and increase student retention by 60%.
